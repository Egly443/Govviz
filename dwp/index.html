Govviz / Work & Pensions

Department for Work & Pensions

Whether claimants get decisions promptly, whether work coaches have manageable caseloads, and whether the system is losing money to fraud and error.

Approximate Total Managed Expenditure: £290bn (HM Treasury, Public Spending Statistics (PESA), 2025–26).

Tracked indicators

  • PIP end-to-end clearance time — Median days, new claims (DWP Personal Independence Payment statistics)
  • Work coach caseload ratio — UC claimants per work coach (full-time equivalent) (DWP / NAO work coach capacity reporting)
  • Fraud & error in benefit spend — Overpayments as % of total benefit expenditure (DWP fraud and error in the benefit system)
  • Outstanding UC mandatory reconsiderations — Open cases awaiting reconsideration (DWP mandatory reconsiderations statistics)
  • Population aged 65+ — % of total population (pension pressure) (World Bank (UN Population))
  • Old-age dependency ratio — People 65+ per 100 of working age (World Bank (UN))
  • Female labour participation — % of female population 15+ (World Bank (ILO))
  • Income inequality (Gini) — Gini index (0 = equal, 100 = unequal) — UK vs Germany & France (World Bank)
  • Youth unemployment — Unemployment, ages 15–24 (%) (World Bank (ILO))
