HM Treasury

The economy and the public finances: living standards people actually feel, the cost of the national debt, and how much tax is taken in total. Sized here by debt interest, the Treasury's largest direct outlay.

Approximate Total Managed Expenditure: £105bn (HM Treasury, Public Spending Statistics (PESA), 2025–26).

Tracked indicators

  • Real GDP per head — Chained-volume £ per person (ONS quarterly national accounts)
  • Inflation vs pay growth — Annual % change: CPI vs average weekly earnings (ONS consumer prices & average weekly earnings)
  • Food prices — CPI food & non-alcoholic drink price index (2015 = 100) (ONS Consumer Prices Index — food & non-alcoholic beverages (D7BU))
  • Public sector net debt — % of GDP (ONS / OBR public sector finances)
  • National debt in cash terms — Public sector net debt ex banks, £ trillion (ONS / OBR public sector finances)
  • Total tax take — Tax as % of GDP (the tax burden) (World Bank (IMF GFS))
  • Debt interest — Debt interest as % of government revenue (World Bank (IMF GFS))
  • Regional divide — London GVA per head as a multiple of the UK average (ONS, Regional gross value added (balanced) per head)
  • Unemployment rate — % of the economically active, aged 16+ (ONS Labour Force Survey)
  • GDP growth — Real GDP, annual % change (World Bank)
  • Employment rate — Employment-to-population, 15+ (%) (World Bank (ILO))
  • Labour force participation — % of population 15+ (World Bank (ILO))
  • Investment — Gross capital formation, % of GDP (World Bank)
  • Trade openness — Trade (exports + imports), % of GDP (World Bank)
  • Gross savings — % of GDP (World Bank)
  • Current account balance — % of GDP (World Bank)
  • GNI per head (PPP) — Gross national income per person, PPP $ — UK vs Germany & France (World Bank)
  • Real household income per head — Real households' disposable income, £ per person (ONS real households' disposable income)
  • Productivity — Output per hour worked, index (ONS labour productivity)
  • Direct vs indirect tax — % of government revenue: income/profit taxes vs goods/services taxes (World Bank (IMF GFS))
  • Budget deficit — Public sector net borrowing, % of GDP (ONS / OBR public sector finances)
