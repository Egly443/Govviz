Govviz / Education

Department for Education

How the schools system is performing on the measures that families and economists both care about: outcomes, retention, financial sustainability, and pipeline.

Approximate Total Managed Expenditure: £95bn (HM Treasury, Public Spending Statistics (PESA), 2025–26).

Tracked indicators

  • Disadvantaged attainment gap — KS4 disadvantage gap index (higher = wider gap) (DfE, Explore Education Statistics (KS4 performance))
  • Persistent absence from school — % of pupils missing 10%+ of sessions, state schools, England (DfE, Pupil absence in schools in England (EES))
  • Early-career teacher attrition — % leaving state schools within 5 years of qualifying (DfE School Workforce in England (EES))
  • Local Authority DSG deficits — Cumulative high-needs block deficit, £ billion (DfE / LGA dedicated schools grant deficit data)
  • Teacher training recruitment — Postgraduate ITT enrolments vs target (%) (DfE ITT Census (EES))
  • Spending per primary pupil — Govt expenditure per pupil, % of GDP per head — UK vs Germany & France (World Bank (UNESCO))
  • Education spending (% of GDP) — Government expenditure on education, % of GDP (World Bank (UNESCO))
  • Pupil–teacher ratio (primary) — Pupils per teacher, primary schools (World Bank (UNESCO))
  • University participation — Tertiary enrolment, % gross (World Bank (UNESCO))
